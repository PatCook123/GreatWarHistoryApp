Background of the Great War


The Great War, or the First World War, was the first truly global conflict in human history. Its theatres involved 32 countries fielding more than 70 million military personnel, and took the lives of between 8.5 and 15 million soldiers. Fighting spread across Europe and stretched from the southern plains of Africa, to the deserts of the Middle-East, all the way to the frigid Pacific coasts of northeast Asia.

The Allies, otherwise known as the Triple Entente, included Britain, France, Russia, and later Italy and the United States which fought against the Central Powers, Germany, Austria-Hungary, the Ottoman Empire, and Bulgaria.

The war lasted from August 1914 to November 1918 and sparked further conflicts which did not resolve until the early 1920's.

Guide

The Great War was a complex and often misunderstood period in history. It involved a myriad of battles, key figures, human sufferings, and political events. The development of this timeline was not in the effort to cover all the events and nuances over those four-and-a-half years. Instead, it was created to provide a base understanding of and hopefully encouragement to learn more about the First World War and to, in some small way, help remove its title as the 'Forgotten War'.

You are encouraged to follow along on the timeline in chronological order as many of the events build on each other. Take your time, explore the linked articles, and think critically of the parallels of this history in our own time. Happy learning!

Timeline Navigation

To move through the timeline and onto the next event, click on the right-hand side of the timeline's current article. To move more freely, the events on the timeline themselves can also be clicked. Events on the timeline are organized by type and theatre to keep things a little more organized. Zoom buttons on the left-hand side of the timeline allow you to see a larger or smaller space of time.
